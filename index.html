Training Philosophy

True fitness is not just about how you look, but how you feel, function, and live your daily life.

My approach to fitness is built on three foundational principles:

01 Individualization

No two bodies are alike. I create completely customized programs based on your unique biomechanics, lifestyle, preferences, and goals. Cookie-cutter programs produce cookie-cutter results.

02 Progressive Methodology

Sustainable results come from intelligent progression. I systematically challenge your body through strategic programming that builds upon each success while minimizing plateaus and injuries.

03 Balanced Approach

True fitness integrates movement, nutrition, recovery, and mindset. My programs address all aspects of health to create lasting change that enhances your entire life, not just your physical appearance.

I believe fitness should enhance your life, not consume it. My goal is to help you build a strong, capable body that supports your ambitions and brings you confidence, energy, and joy.

Understanding Your BMI

Under 18.5 - Underweight

May indicate nutritional deficiencies or other health issues. Focus on balanced nutrition with adequate calories.

18.5 to 24.9 - Normal weight

Associated with good health and lower risk of chronic diseases. Maintain a balanced diet and regular physical activity.

25 to 29.9 - Overweight

May increase risk of heart disease, diabetes, and other conditions. Consider moderate weight loss through diet and exercise.

30 and above - Obese

Significantly increases health risks and may require medical intervention. Focus on sustainable lifestyle changes.

BMI is a screening tool and not a diagnostic of body fatness or health. It doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or overall body composition. Always consult with fitness professionals for a comprehensive assessment.
